Harumanis Mango growth Modeling

In modern Malaysian agrotechnology, matching nutrient distribution with physiological growth phases is critical. The signature Harumanis mango (Mangifera indica L.) exhibits a highly characteristic sigmoidal curve as it amasses fresh biomass. By modeling total fruit mass as a function of Days After Full Bloom (DAFB), agrotechnologists can calculate the first derivative $f'(x)$ to find the precise moment of maximum daily weight gain. This helps optimize expensive automated fertigation cycles in smart greenhouses.

🇲🇾 Data Validation & Standards:

  • Maximum mature fruit size: ~450 grams
  • Total maturation timeframe: 110 Days after flowering
  • Dataset calibration reference: Department of Agriculture (DOA) Malaysia (doa.gov.my)
  • Physiological indices source: MARDI Agrotech Monograph Series (mardi.gov.my)

∫ Calculus Formulation

Growth Function: Logistic curve
$$f(x) = \frac{L}{1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}}$$

Asymptote ($L$)

450g (Max Weight)

Growth Coefficient ($k$)

0.08 /day

Inflection ($x_0$)

55 DAFB

Derivative (Rate of Change): Inst. growth rate
$$f'(x) = \frac{L \cdot k \cdot e^{-k(x - x_0)}}{\left(1 + e^{-k(x - x_0)}\right)^2}$$

The derivative $f'(x)$ outputs the **Instantaneous Mass Growth Rate** measured specifically in grams per day ($g/\text{day}$).
